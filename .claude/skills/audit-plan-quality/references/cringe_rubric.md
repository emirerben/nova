# Plan-item cringe rubric

This rubric grades a single content-plan item — `{theme, idea, filming_suggestion,
rationale}` produced for a specific creator persona. The one question behind every
score: **would a real creator happily film and post this, or would they cringe and
close the app?** Nova films real life — there is no studio, no graphics team, no
stock footage, no re-enactments. If you can't picture the actual footage of *this
person*, the item is wrong.

You are given the persona (summary, pillars, tone, audience) and the plan's items
together, so judge each item *in the context of who it's for* and *against the
other items* (catch repetition).

## Score (1–5) — overall postability

- **5** — A real, specific, filmable moment from this creator's actual life. You
  can see the footage. Peer-to-peer voice ("here's the spot I always go"). The
  kind of thing they'd post without a second thought.
- **4** — Solid and filmable, maybe slightly generic but clearly fits this person.
- **3** — Borderline. Filmable but bland, or fits the persona loosely, or leans on
  a tired format with no specific hook.
- **2** — Mostly cringe or barely filmable: forced framing, abstract lesson with a
  thin visual, or generic advice this person has no special claim to.
- **1** — The "close the app" item: thought-leadership monologue, a metaphor
  stretched into a business takeaway, or something this creator plainly cannot film.

Anything scoring < 4 is treated as flagged in the report. Independently, set every
failure flag that applies (an item can score 3 with one flag, or 1 with several).

## Failure flags (set all that apply)

- **thought_leadership** — "what [X] taught me about [Y]", "[everyday thing] is just
  like [business/life lesson]", forced-insight framing. The canonical offender:
  *"What the Champions League final taught me about strategic risk assessment in
  business."* → fix is the real moment: *"my matchday ritual — come watch the final
  with me at my spot."*
- **tenuous_metaphor** — stretches an event, hobby, or object into an abstract
  business / mindset / productivity takeaway it doesn't naturally carry.
- **talking_head_abstract** — a monologue about an abstract concept (strategy,
  leadership, mindset, "analytical thinking") with no real footage behind it.
- **generic_motivational** — motivational or self-help advice not tied to something
  *this specific person* actually does, makes, or visits. Could be posted by anyone.
- **not_filmable** — you cannot picture the literal footage: needs a studio, graphics,
  stock, re-enactment, or describes a concept rather than a scene.
- **unrealistic_for_persona** — assumes access, money, skill, location, or people this
  creator clearly doesn't have based on the persona (e.g. a home cook "touring three
  Michelin kitchens", a student "flying to Tokyo for the weekend").
- **cheesy_hook** — try-hard, cringe, or salesy framing/voice: aspirational-guru tone,
  a LinkedIn lecture, fake urgency, or a hook no real person would say out loud.
- **repetitive** — substantially the same idea as another item in the same plan
  (same concept reworded across days). Judge against the other items shown.
- **abstract_theme** — the `theme` names an abstract concept ("Analytical Thinking",
  "Strategic Insights") instead of a concrete recurring thing the creator does. This
  flag usually points upstream at the *persona* (an abstract pillar), not the plan.

## Calibration examples

PASS (score 5, no flags):
> persona: a home cook in Lisbon · idea: "the 3-ingredient weeknight pasta I make
> when I get home late — filmed start to plate in my actual kitchen"

FAIL (score 1, thought_leadership + talking_head_abstract):
> persona: a startup founder · idea: "What scoring a goal in my Sunday league taught
> me about closing enterprise deals"

FAIL (score 2, generic_motivational + cheesy_hook):
> persona: a data analyst · idea: "5 mindset shifts that will 10x your productivity
> (talking to camera)"

FAIL (score 2, unrealistic_for_persona):
> persona: a university student on a budget · idea: "a weekend in Dubai — luxury
> hotel room tour and fine dining"

Pass threshold: avg ≥ 4.0
