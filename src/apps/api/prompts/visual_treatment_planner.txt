Editorial policy:
- A montage compresses hook/context/example evidence. Use it only with at least 3
  relevant assets. Prefer 3-10 assets and a 1.2-6.0 second window.
- A text card isolates a transcript-backed statistic, quote, key argument, or true
  section transition. Copy or tightly condense the spoken claim; never invent facts.
- Use purpose "section_item" for an explicit item in an announced structure: numbered
  subtopics, rankings, steps, lessons, reasons, or named list items. When the speaker
  announces a finite structure (for example "four headings" or "top five"), emit one
  section_item card for every clearly spoken item, up to eight items.
- A section_item card contains ONLY the spoken ordinal/number and item title. Do not
  include its translation, definition, evidence, or explanatory sentence. Preserve the
  spoken ordering even when the card normalizes "birinci"/"first" to "1".
- Time a section_item from the ordinal through the title and end it before the speaker
  starts the definition or explanation. The underlying talking-head footage must return
  for that explanation. Do not turn the structure-preview sentence itself into a card.
- A bare opening list used as a hook is not automatically a structured sequence. It needs
  an announced order plus individually introduced items later in the transcript.
- "footage" records that an important passage should deliberately remain untreated.
- Cut on sentence/keyword/beat boundaries. Do not change visuals on a fixed timer.
- Use weak/repetitive footage and time since the last meaningful treatment only as
  modifiers. Speech pace, text load, sentence importance, and music energy matter;
  never manufacture a pattern interrupt merely because time elapsed.
- Avoid consecutive text cards, repetitive treatment, and covering more than a third
  of the edit. Strong footage should remain footage.
- Beat-only input may justify a montage but never a semantic text card.
- Be selective. Zero treatments is valid.
