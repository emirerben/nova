SUCCESS_FACTORS

Every idea must be a real, filmable moment from THIS creator's actual life —
something a camera can literally capture them doing, in a place they actually
are, with objects or people that are actually there. If you cannot picture the
footage, the idea is wrong. Nova turns real-life clips into short videos: there
is no studio, no graphics team, no stock footage, no re-enactments.

NEVER write "thought-leadership" or forced-insight ideas — they read as cringe
and make a creator close the app the moment they see one. Specifically banned:
- "what [X] taught me about [Y]" / "[everyday thing] is just like [business or
  life lesson]" framing — e.g. NOT "what the Champions League final taught me
  about strategic risk assessment in business".
- tenuous metaphors that stretch an event, hobby, or object into an abstract
  business / mindset / productivity takeaway.
- talking-head monologues about abstract concepts (strategy, leadership,
  mindset, "analytical thinking") with no real footage behind them.
- generic motivational or self-help advice not tied to something this specific
  person actually does, makes, or visits.

Instead, anchor every idea in a concrete activity, place, object, or moment:
what they make, where they go, what they show, who they are with. Keep the voice
peer-to-peer ("here's the spot I always go", "come do this with me") — never
aspirational-guru, never a LinkedIn lecture. If a persona pillar is abstract
(e.g. "analytical thinking"), translate it into something filmable from their
real life rather than narrating the concept.

Build a $horizon_days-day content plan. Return ONLY a JSON object:

{
  "items": [
    {
      "day_index": 1,
      "theme": "short label for the day's content pillar",
      "idea": "one concrete video concept, specific to this creator",
      "filming_suggestion": "a practical tip for shooting it (setting, shot, time of day)",
      "rationale": "one short sentence, shown to the creator as 'why this works': why this idea fits them AND which proven lever it pulls (name a success factor above, e.g. save-worthy, hook in the first 2s, relatable one-liner).",
      "edit_format": "one of: montage | talking_head | day_vlog | single_hero — the edit shape this idea should become (see rules)"
    }
  ]
}

Rules:
- Provide one item per day from day_index 1 up to $horizon_days. day_index values are unique and within 1..$horizon_days.
- Rotate across the creator's content pillars so the plan feels varied, not repetitive. Cluster a couple of related ideas when it helps a mini-series, but never repeat the same idea twice.
- Ground every idea in the persona (and the events, if any). No generic "post a get-ready-with-me" filler unless it fits this person.
- Every idea must be physically filmable real life — name what the camera sees. No abstract "lessons", no forced metaphors, no thought-leadership monologues (see above).
- theme must name a concrete, recurring thing this creator actually does — not an abstract concept (NOT "Analytical Thinking", "Strategic Insights").
- Front-load the strongest, most postable ideas in the first 7 days (that is the activation week).
- theme and idea must be non-empty. filming_suggestion should be practical and short.
- rationale: one short sentence per item, naming a real lever — not generic praise. It is shown to the creator.
- edit_format: choose the shape that matches how the idea is filmed:
    - talking_head — the creator speaks to camera and that voice carries the video (a take, a story, a tip, an explainer). B-roll may cut over it.
    - day_vlog — several clips from across one day/outing, shown in time order (morning to night, a trip, "a day in my life").
    - single_hero — one strong continuous shot stands on its own (one cooked dish, one view, one outfit), minimal cutting.
    - montage — default: multiple short clips cut to music with text. Use when none of the above clearly fits.
  When unsure, use montage. Match the filming_suggestion: if it says "to camera", that is talking_head; if it spans a day, that is day_vlog.
- Output valid JSON and nothing else. No markdown.
